## Service overview

TikTok is the short-video app China's ByteDance launched on September 20, 2016. ByteDance was founded in 2012 by Zhang Yiming and Liang Rubo, among others; per its official site, it now operates in roughly 120 cities worldwide with over 150,000 employees, and its board includes well-known investors like Coatue, General Atlantic, Sequoia Capital, KKR, and SoftBank Vision Fund. Globally, TikTok itself is run by TikTok Ltd., incorporated in the Cayman Islands and based in Singapore and Los Angeles (per Wikipedia).

:::fact
On September 14, 2025, a framework agreement for the US business was announced, and on January 22, 2026, a new company, "TikTok USDS Joint Venture LLC," began operating as the entity dedicated to the US business. Per aggregated Wikipedia reporting, ownership stakes are split with Oracle, MGX Fund Management, and Silver Lake each holding 15%, ByteDance reduced to 19.9%, and the remaining 35.1% split between Michael Dell's investment entity and Vastmere Strategic Investments. The entity's own official site states it covers five areas: data protection, algorithm security, trust and safety, software assurance, and interoperability. User scale is reported at 2 billion downloads by April 2020 and 1 billion monthly active users by September 2021.
:::

:::pull
The same "For You" feed is run by ByteDance-affiliated TikTok Ltd. almost everywhere in the world, and by a separate company in the US alone. One app, with the nationality of its business operations split apart — there's no precedent for this structure.
:::

## UX analysis

The core of TikTok's UX is the "For You" feed itself, which doesn't depend on follows or friend relationships.

- **A feed optimized without requiring follows.** Per aggregated Wikipedia reporting, the feed weights in-viewing behavioral signals — watch time, replays, scroll speed — more heavily than the follow-based feeds of earlier social networks.
- **The short-video format became an industry standard.** Major rivals — Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts — have implemented similar formats, making TikTok's feed design a reference point for the industry.
- **Creator monetization and commerce sit in the same screen.** Watching a video and buying through TikTok Shop complete inside the same app, deliberately blurring the line between content consumption and commerce.
- **The UX impact of the US-only operator change is still unknown.** The app's look and core functionality are expected to stay the same, but how a change in the entity deciding algorithm tuning and US feature rollout ripples into the experience will only become clear through actual operation.

The "For You" recommendation feature is described as an AI-driven feed weighting in-viewing behavioral data over follow relationships. Within the same ByteDance group, related products such as TikTok Shop and CapCut are also operated.
:::

:::guess
Separating the global app entity (TikTok Ltd.) from a US-only joint venture handling algorithm and data governance (USDS JV) looks like a design meant to answer the US government's repeatedly stated concerns about data sovereignty and algorithm safety by carving US governance out into its own legal entity. Reducing ByteDance's stake to 19.9% while preserving product continuity — the same For You feed, the same UI — reads as a deliberate separation of effective business control from product experience, an attempt to satisfy both US political concerns and a globally consistent product at once. Whether this structure actually works in practice depends on how much independent decision-making the US-only joint venture ends up exercising.
:::

## Business model

TikTok's revenue rests on two pillars: advertising and commerce fees from TikTok Shop.

:::fact
Per aggregated Wikipedia reporting, advertising revenue reached $4 billion in 2021 and was projected at $14.15 billion in 2023 (up from $9.89 billion in 2022). The same source notes US ad monetization at roughly $0.31 per hour of viewing, about a third of Facebook's rate. TikTok Shop launched first in the UK, with a US rollout announced in October 2022, positioned as a commerce feature that lets users go from watching a video to buying on the spot inside the same app.
:::

:::guess
A lower ad rate than rivals suggests TikTok's monetization hasn't yet matured to match its user scale. The investment in TikTok Shop looks aimed at growing commerce fees as a separate revenue source to offset that gap. The 2026 restructuring into a joint venture for the US business may add short-term operational cost and decision-making complexity, but it plausibly also removes the risk of a US market exit and signals continuity to advertisers and creators alike.
:::

The same app, the same For You feed, run by ByteDance affiliates almost everywhere and by a separate joint venture in the US alone — what this dissection of TikTok reveals is a structural compromise with no real precedent: keep the product consistent while splitting the nationality of business operations by region. Whether that compromise holds up is something only the joint venture's operating track record can show.
